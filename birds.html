The Amazon Rainforest

Many animals use several mechanisms to either survive from the heat, find food and defend themselves from predators. For example, Macaws communicate with a range of loud calls, which serve a dual purpose. One, it signals a warning call to other macaws in the area that there is a predator in the vicinity. The other purpose is to alert predators that they have been spotted and are being watched.

The most commonly known mechanisms animals use in the Rainforest is camouflage to hide from predators, any fur, feathers, or extra skin to protect them from the heat, and also having the inteligence to make tools and objects to help them survive (For example, orangutans can craft tools from natural items in the forest to help them survive).
