The Sahara Desert

Life in the Sahara desert is very harsh and extremely hot during the daytime. It is scorching hot in the summer season with a temperature above 50Â°C.

Those few animals that live in the Sahara desert is well adapted to the harsh conditions prevailing there. They are mostly nocturnal and so only come out during the night time and remains underground at day time due to avoid excessive water loss. They are capable of staying alive by utilizing the little amount of water they get by metabolizing their body fats, by feeding on succulent plants like cactus, and by taking the blood and meat from their prey.
